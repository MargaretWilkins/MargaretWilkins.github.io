Margaret Wilkins

Musical Works from Margaret Wilkins

Margaret Lucy Wilkins - Selected Works Orchestral Works: - Dance Variations (1973) - 11’. Commissioned by The University of St. Andrews Chamber Orchestra, funded by Scottish Arts Council. Instrumentation: harpsichord/strings: 6 6 3 2 1. Description: A lively and dynamic orchestral piece featuring dance-inspired variations, showcasing the interplay between harpsichord and strings. - Hymn to Creation (1973) - 9’. Instrumentation: 3333/433/1T 3P/7 6 5 4 3. Description: A contemplative and evocative orchestral hymn, celebrating the beauty of creation with rich harmonies and melodic lines. - Music of the Spheres (1975) - 17’. Commissioned by BBC. Instrumentation: 333(+ bass cl) 3/4331/hrp/1T 3P/12 10 8 6. Description: Inspired by celestial harmonies, this orchestral work explores ethereal textures and grand orchestrations. - Revelations of the Seven Angels (1988) - c. 50’. Instrumentation: solo sop/cathedral choir/string quartet/orchestra: 2131/2431/hrp/guitar/celeste-harpsichord/2 descant recorders/2P/strings: 12 10 8 8 6. Description: An ambitious and spiritually charged work, featuring a solo soprano, choir, and various instrumental groups, portraying the biblical theme of divine revelations. - Symphony (1989) - 22’. Instrumentation: 3333/4331/1T 2P/16 14 12 10 8. Description: A symphonic masterpiece with bold orchestral colors and intricate thematic development, showcasing the composer's command of orchestration. - Musica Angelorum (1991) - 10’. 30”. Commissioned by Goldberg Ensemble. Instrumentation: solo strings: 4 3 2 2 1. Description: A captivating and lyrical work for solo strings, evoking the image of angels playing celestial music, featuring delicate and expressive passages. Instrumental Ensemble: - Orpheus (1973) - 14’. Instrumentation: violin & piano. Description: A virtuosic and expressive duo for violin and piano, inspired by the mythical figure of Orpheus. - Burnt Sienna: Etude for string trio (1974) - 9’. Instrumentation: violin/viola/cello. Description: A set of studies exploring various dramatic sonorities obtainable from a string trio, featuring intricate interplay between the instruments. - Circus (1975) - 13’. Instrumentation: flute/clarinet/bass clarinet/horn/trumpet/violin/double bass/1 percussion/piano. Description: A lively and colorful ensemble piece with circus-themed elements, incorporating imaginative instrumental effects. - Aspects of Night (1981) - 10’. 30”. Instrumentation: recorder/s (1 player) & guitar. Description: An evocative and atmospheric work for recorder and guitar, capturing the essence of nighttime sounds and moods. - Circuit for bassoon & piano (1986) - 3’. 30”. Description: A brief and lively piece showcasing the agility and expressive capabilities of the bassoon, accompanied by the piano. - Epistola da San Marco (1987) - 10’. Instrumentation: brass band. Description: A powerful and resonant essay for brass band, featuring majestic trumpet fanfares and thunderous passages. - Réve, Réveil, Révelation, Réverbérations (1988) - 13’. Instrumentation: alto flute/clarinet/bass clarinet/bassoon/guitar/harp/vibraphone/marimba/DX7.2/4 violins/cello/double bass. Description: A striking and inventive work with an array of unusual instrumental combinations, exploring rich timbres and resonances. - A Baker’s Dozen (1994) - 1’. Instrumentation: descant recorder & piano. Description: A short and delightful duo for descant recorder and piano, featuring playful and melodic passages. - Trompettes de Mort (2003) - c. 10’. Instrumentation: 2 trumpets, horn, trombone, tuba, piano. Description: A powerful and dramatic work for brass quintet, showcasing the bold and resonant colors of the brass instruments. Solo Instruments: - A Dance to the Music of Time (1980) - 10’. 30”. Solo harpsichord. Description: A delicate and intricate solo piece for the harpsichord, featuring a finely woven subject and lively rhythmic pulses. - Deus ex Machina (1982) - 5’. Solo organ. Description: A captivating and virtuosic work for solo organ, featuring dramatic and expressive passages. - Study in Black & White No.1 (1983) - 6’. Solo piano. Description: A compelling and powerful piano study, exploring contrasting elements in black and white. - Study in Black & White No.2 (1992) - 3’. 30”. Solo piano. Description: A surprising and inventive piano study, showcasing the composer's creative use of contrasting elements. - Study in Black & White No.3 (1995) - 4’. 30”. Solo piano. Description: The third piano study in the series, featuring expressive and melodic phrases contrasted with dynamic rhythms. Electroacoustic Works: - Music for an Exhibition (1970) - musique concrète; sound installation. Description: A unique and immersive sound installation, exploring the possibilities of musique concrète. - Sci-Fi (1973) - sound installation. Description: A futuristic and experimental sound installation, created for a Science Fiction Exhibition. - KANAL (1990) - 60’- 90’. A multi-media environmental event. Description: An intriguing and immersive multi-media event, blending words, music, movement, and light in an abstract and captivating fashion. - Stringsing (1992) - 18’. 15”. Electroacoustic music for dance/stereo DAT. Description: An engaging and dynamic electroacoustic work, specially composed for a dance performance. Choral Works: - Three Skelmanthorpe Carols (1980) - 6’. Male voice choir: 2T/2B. Description: A set of charming and festive carols, perfect for male voice choir performances during Christmas. - Gitanjali (1981) - 16’. 30”. Minimum 16 voices: 4 soprano/4 alto/4 tenor/4 Bass. Description: A choral masterpiece, setting verses from Rabindranath Tagore's Gitanjali, featuring lush harmonies and expressive melodies. - Ring Out, Wild Bells (1997) - 8’. Written for the Vatican Millennium Commission. Unison voices/organ. Description: A celebratory and uplifting choral piece, written for a special occasion. Selected Works for Students, Young Musicians, and Amateur Musicians: - Pirouette for solo oboe - 3’. Description: A delightful and spirited solo piece for oboe, inspired by the graceful movement of a pirouette. - Pas de Quatre for 4 oboes - 5’. Description: A lively and engaging quartet for four oboes, with the instruments "buzzing like bees" in playful passages. - Playtime (1985) - 16’. Children’s voices/flute/clarinet/trumpet/violin/cello/double bass/piano. Description: An imaginative and entertaining ensemble piece, featuring children's voices and various instruments, inspired by local children's poems. - Epistola da San Marco (1987) - 10’. Brass band. Description: An impressive and powerful work for brass band, perfect for showcasing the strengths of brass instruments. - KANAL (1990) - 60’- 90’. A multi-media environmental event. Description: An exciting and immersive multi-media experience, providing young musicians with a chance to participate in a theatrical and innovative performance. - Ring Out, Wild Bells (1997) - 8’. Written for the Vatican Millennium Commission. Unison voices/organ. Description: An accessible and celebratory choral piece, suitable for young or amateur choirs. - Rituelle (1999) - c. 16’. 7 mobile groups: 5 flutes/5 clarinets/4 horns/3 trumpets/3 trombones/1 flute, 1 clarinet, 1 trumpet, 1 trombone/2 timps. + 1 tubular bells. Description: An intriguing and spatially dynamic work, designed for multiple ensembles and perambulating groups.
